<p><p>Bike-sharing services are primarily divided into two types: dockless and station-based markets. In dockless systems, users can pick up and drop off bikes anywhere within a designated area, offering greater flexibility and convenience. In contrast, station-based systems require users to access bikes from specific docking stations and return them to designated locations, providing more control over bike distribution and maintenance. Both models cater to different urban needs, promoting eco-friendly transportation and reducing congestion in cities.</p></p>
