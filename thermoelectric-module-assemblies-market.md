<p><p>Thermoelectric module assemblies are categorized into three main types based on their cooling methods. Air-cooling utilizes fans or convection to dissipate heat, making it suitable for applications with moderate thermal loads. Liquid-cooling employs fluids to transfer heat more efficiently, ideal for high-performance systems requiring efficient temperature control. Direct contact involves the thermoelectric modules being in direct contact with heat sinks or other materials, facilitating immediate heat transfer for rapid cooling. Each type serves distinct applications in various industries.</p></p>
