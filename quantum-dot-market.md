<p><p>Quantum dots (QDs) are semiconductor nanocrystals that exhibit unique optical and electronic properties due to their size and shape. These nanostructures are typically composed of elements like cadmium, selenium, or indium, and can emit specific wavelengths of light when excited. Innovations in display technologies, such as QLED (Quantum Dot Light Emitting Diode), are propelling the adoption of quantum dot technology to enhance color accuracy and energy efficiency. Furthermore, advancements in the development of quantum dot solar cells are poised to accelerate their penetration in renewable energy applications. Emerging trends include the integration of quantum dots in lighting solutions and augmented reality devices, reflecting a shift towards more immersive experiences in electronic products.
