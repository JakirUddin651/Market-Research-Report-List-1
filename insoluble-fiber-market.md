<p><p>Insoluble fiber is a type of dietary fiber that does not dissolve in water. Common sources of insoluble fiber include whole grains, nuts, seeds, and the skins of fruits and vegetables. As consumer awareness of health and nutrition increases, the demand for insoluble fiber has surged, contributing to the growth of the market.</p><p>The Insoluble Fiber Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 15.20% during the forecast period. Additionally, the functional food sector is integrating insoluble fiber into various products to cater to health-focused consumers, further boosting market growth. Emerging trends include the incorporation of insoluble fiber in snack foods and beverages, as well as advancements in processing technology to enhance fiber's health benefits. Overall, the market is poised for significant expansion as more consumers seek to incorporate dietary fibers into their diets for improved health and wellness.</p></p>
