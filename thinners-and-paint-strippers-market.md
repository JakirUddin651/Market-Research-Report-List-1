<p><p>The thinners and paint strippers market comprises various solvents, each serving distinct purposes. Mineral spirits are commonly used for oil-based paints, providing a mild odor and effective thinning. Acetone offers fast evaporation, ideal for removing nail polish and adhesives. Turpentine is derived from pine and favored in traditional applications. Naphtha serves as a versatile paint thinner, while toluene is effective for cleaning and diluting. Methyl Ethyl Ketone (MEK) is known for strong solvency, and Dimethylformamide (DMF) is utilized in industrial applications. 2-Butoxyethanol is valued for its ability to penetrate coatings efficiently. Each solvent has unique properties that cater to specific needs in the painting and coating industries.</p></p>
