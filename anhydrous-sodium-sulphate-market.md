<p><p>The Anhydrous Sodium Sulphate market is primarily divided into two types: byproduct and natural product sodium sulfate. Byproduct sodium sulfate is obtained during the manufacturing processes of other chemicals, often utilizing waste materials, making it an economical choice. In contrast, natural product sodium sulfate is sourced directly from natural deposits, such as evaporated salt lakes, and is typically of high purity. Both types serve various industries, including detergents, glass, and textiles, facilitating diverse applications in different sectors.</p></p>
