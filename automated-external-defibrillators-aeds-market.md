<p><p>Automated External Defibrillators (AEDs) are portable devices that deliver an electric shock to restore normal heart rhythm during cardiac emergencies. The market for AEDs is divided into two primary types: semi-automated and fully automated. Semi-automated AEDs require user intervention to deliver a shock after analyzing the heart's rhythm, while fully automated AEDs automatically assess the heart rhythm and deliver shocks without any user action. These devices improve accessibility and encourage public involvement in emergency response.</p></p>
