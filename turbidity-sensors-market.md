<p><p>The turbidity sensors market is primarily divided into two types: analog and digital turbidity sensors. Analog turbidity sensors provide a continuous voltage output proportional to turbidity levels, often requiring manual calibration and interpretation. In contrast, digital turbidity sensors offer enhanced accuracy and ease of use by providing digital readings, often incorporating automated calibration features. Both sensor types are used in various applications, including water quality monitoring, industrial processes, and environmental assessments, ensuring effective measurement of particulate matter in liquids.</p></p>
