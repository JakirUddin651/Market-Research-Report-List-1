<p><p>The metal-ceramic dental bridge market includes several types designed for different clinical needs. Traditional bridges involve anchoring to adjacent teeth for support. Cantilever bridges are used when there is only one adjacent tooth for support. Maryland bridges feature a metal framework with wings that bond to the back of teeth, providing a less invasive option. Implant-supported bridges are attached to dental implants instead of natural teeth, offering a durable and stable solution for patients missing multiple teeth.</p></p>
