<p><p>The tool spindles market is categorized into several types based on their drive mechanisms. Gear-driven spindles utilize gears for high torque and precise control, making them ideal for heavy-duty applications. Belt-driven spindles employ belts for smoother, quieter operations, often used in lighter machining tasks. Direct-driven spindles have motors integrated directly with the spindle, offering high speed and efficiency for precision work. Additionally, other types may include unique designs or hybrid systems to cater to specific industry needs.</p></p>
