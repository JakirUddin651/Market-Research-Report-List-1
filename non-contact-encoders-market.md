<p><p>Non-contact encoders are devices that provide position and speed feedback without making physical contact with the object being measured. <p><p>Non-contact encoders are sensors that detect position or movement without physical contact, enhancing durability and reliability. </p><p>Magnetic encoders use magnetic fields to determine angular position, offering robustness in harsh environments. Capacitive encoders operate based on changes in capacitance, providing high precision with minimal mechanical wear. Optical encoders utilize light reflection or interruption to measure motion, known for their high accuracy. Each type serves varied applications, emphasizing resolution, environmental robustness, and cost-effectiveness in automotive, industrial, and consumer electronics sectors.</p></p>
