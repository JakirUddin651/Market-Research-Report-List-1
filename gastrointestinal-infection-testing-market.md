<p><p>The gastrointestinal infection testing market is segmented into three main types: viral, bacterial, and parasitic infections. Viral gastrointestinal infections are primarily caused by pathogens like norovirus and rotavirus, leading to symptoms such as diarrhea and vomiting. Bacterial infections, often from bacteria like Salmonella and E. coli, can arise from contaminated food or water, causing severe gastrointestinal distress. Parasitic infections, caused by organisms like Giardia and Cryptosporidium, typically result from contaminated sources, leading to chronic digestive issues. Each segment requires specific testing methods for accurate diagnosis and treatment.</p></p>
