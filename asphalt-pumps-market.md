<p><p>The asphalt pumps market features several types, each designed for specific applications. Gear pumps utilize interlocking gears to move asphalt efficiently, while screw pumps use helical screws for a smooth, continuous flow, ideal for high-viscosity materials. Rotary vane pumps operate with sliding vanes that create vacuum pressure for precise metering and transfer. Other types may include peristaltic and diaphragm pumps, catering to diverse industry needs. Each pump type offers unique advantages depending on pressure requirements, flow rates, and material characteristics.</p></p>
