<p><p>Non-dispersive infrared (NDIR) CO2 sensors detect carbon dioxide levels by measuring the absorption of infrared light at specific wavelengths. The market is categorized into single-channel and dual-channel types. Single-channel sensors utilize one wavelength for measurement, making them cost-effective but less accurate in varying conditions. Dual-channel sensors employ two wavelengths, enhancing accuracy by compensating for potential interference and variations in environmental factors. This results in improved performance and reliability in diverse applications, from indoor air quality monitoring to industrial processes.</p></p>
