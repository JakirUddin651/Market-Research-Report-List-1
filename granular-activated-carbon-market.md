<p><p>Granular Activated Carbon (GAC) is a highly porous form of carbon processed to have small, granular particles, primarily used for adsorption of contaminants in water, air, and gas purification processes. Its high surface area and adsorption capacity make it effective in removing organic compounds, chlorine, and other impurities. GAC is widely used in industries such as water treatment, food and beverage, pharmaceuticals, and air purification.</p><p>The Granular Activated Carbon Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing demand for clean water, stringent environmental regulations, and rising awareness about pollution control. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.8% during the forecast period. Key factors contributing to this growth include urbanization, industrialization, and the need for advanced filtration systems in emerging economies.</p><p>Recent trends in the GAC market include the development of sustainable and cost-effective production methods, such as using renewable raw materials like coconut shells and wood. Additionally, advancements in reactivation technologies are enhancing the reusability of GAC, reducing costs and environmental impact. The growing adoption of GAC in emerging applications, such as biogas purification and gold recovery, is also driving market expansion. As industries prioritize sustainability and compliance with environmental standards, the demand for granular activated carbon is expected to continue rising.</p></p>
