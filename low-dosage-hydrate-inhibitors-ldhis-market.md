<p><strong>The Low-Dosage Hydrate Inhibitors (LDHIs) Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Anti-Agglomerate (AA) Inhibitors</li><li>Kinetic Hydrate Inhibitors (KHI)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Low-Dosage Hydrate Inhibitors (LDHIs) are chemical additives used in the oil and gas industry to prevent hydrate formation in pipelines and equipment. The market for LDHIs comprises two main types: Anti-Agglomerate (AA) inhibitors, which prevent hydrate particles from clumping together, and Kinetic Hydrate Inhibitors (KHI), which delay hydrate formation by slowing down the nucleation and growth processes. Both types are essential for enhancing flow assurance while minimizing chemical usage and operational costs in hydrocarbon transportation.</p></p>
