<p><p>The elastomeric impression materials market comprises various types, each with unique properties. Addition silicone, known for its high accuracy and stability, is widely used in dental applications. Condensation silicone offers good detail but may be less precise over time. Polyether is valued for its hydrophilicity and dimensional stability, making it suitable for moist environments. Polysulfide provides excellent elasticity and tear strength but has longer setting times. Additionally, "others" encompasses emerging materials that cater to specific needs within the industry.</p></p>
