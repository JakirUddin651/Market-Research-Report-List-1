<p><p>The DNA modifying enzymes market includes various types that play essential roles in molecular biology and genetic manipulation. Alkaline phosphatases remove phosphate groups, preventing unwanted ligation during cloning. Exonucleases digest nucleic acids from the ends, useful for creating precise DNA fragments. Endonucleases cut at specific internal sites, enabling targeted gene editing. Terminal transferases add nucleotides to the ends of DNA strands, facilitating the modification and extension of DNA molecules. These enzymes are crucial for research, diagnostics, and therapeutic applications.</p></p>
