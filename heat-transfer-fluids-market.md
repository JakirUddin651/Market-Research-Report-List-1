<p><p>The heat transfer fluids market comprises several types, including mineral oils, silicones, aromatics, and glycols. Mineral oils are widely used due to their stability and effective heat transfer properties but can degrade at high temperatures. Silicones offer thermal stability and a broad temperature range, making them suitable for demanding applications. Aromatic fluids provide excellent heat transfer efficiency and thermal stability but may have environmental and health concerns. Glycols, particularly ethylene and propylene glycol, are used in various applications for their low freezing points and good thermal conductivity.</p></p>
